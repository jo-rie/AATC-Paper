\title{Trending assessment for nowcasts, measurements, and forecasts}
\author{Oliver Grothe, Bolin Liu, Jonas Rieger}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
An intuitive and easily accessible information on a quantity measured over time is whether it increases or decreases.
Trending refers to the ability to predict the direction of change for a true variable accurately. 
Thus, an appropriate assessment of the trending ability is crucial for measurements, nowcasts, and forecasts for decision-makers. 
While the current practice of evaluating signals is based on averaging over local deviation measures, we review and introduce trending measures that consider whether the direction of development fits the quantity of interest. 
We formalize the concept of trending for all application areas and present quantitative measures. 
Using bootstrap confidence intervals and exclusion areas, we provide extensions for noisy data and estimation uncertainty.
We review and present new visualization techniques to assess the local trending behaviors and enlarge the scope to probabilistic nowcasts and forecasts. 
We apply the methods to three large data applications: COVID-19-nowcasting, emergency department arrival forecasting, and invasive and non-invasive blood pressure measurements.
\end{abstract}
